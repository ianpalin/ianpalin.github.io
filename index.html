About

I was born in Portland Oregon and raised free range. A lifelong passion for science and tinkering led me to pursue both professionally as an applied physicist/ engineer. Outside of work you will find me hiking, snowboarding, playing guitar, and tending to my oversized collection of house plants.

In undergrad, I was inspired to pursue physics by the sense that it offered me the most to learn and after facing that challenge I couldn't be happier with the choice. Despite my love for physics the only classes that had me hooked to keep going in my free time were my electronics and robotics courses. Going where my attention brought me, I completed my masters in mechanical engineering on the robotics and automation track.

My current work, for the National Laboratory of the Rockies' Hydrogen Sensor Laboratory, focuses on the development and testing of frontier sensing and monitoring methods for hydrogen infrastructure. My role involves developing CV/ML systems, GPU programming, data analytics, and control system design/ implementation.

Selected work.

A representative selection; mix of lab work and personal projects.

CV, ML, GPU acceleration

BOS leak detection

Background-oriented schlieren pipeline for visualizing subtle refractive-index disturbances in high res images, then turning those small shifts into useful measurements with GPU-accelerated analysis and machine learning.

MCAD, ECAD, design iteration

Plant robot

Custom PCB, pump control, display UI, enclosure design, and manufacturing intended iteration for an automated watering pot.

Embedded, Sensor systems, signal management

Wireless sensor network

The ML layer on top of the BOS pipeline — CNN and ResNet models trained to regress actual leak rates from displacement imagery. Getting the validation split right was the main design decision: condition-aware grouping by flow rate and distance to prevent leakage across related clips. Held-out R² of 0.85.

Modeling, ML

Plume estimates

Sensor arrays give you readings at fixed points — this uses Gaussian Process Regression to interpolate those into a full concentration field, then couples the result with the TNO multi-energy method to estimate explosive overpressures from hydrogen releases. Published at ICHS 2023.

Signal management, Electrical design, PLC

Controls and electronics builds

Snoop solution (soap bubbles) is how most field techs check for leaks — watch for bubbles, make a call. No number, no repeatability, no defined uncertainty. This project uses smartphone video and a CNN-to-regression pipeline to change that.
