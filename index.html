About

I was born and raised in Portland Oregon and am currently based in Denver Colorado. A lifelong passion for science and tinkering led me to pursue both professionally as an applied physicist/ engineer. Outside of work you will find me hiking, snowboarding, playing guitar, and tending to my oversized collection of house plants.

In undergrad, I was inspired to pursue physics by the sense that it offered me the most to learn and after facing that challenge I couldn't be happier with the choice. The undergrad classed that had me hooked to keep going in my free time were my electronics and robotics courses. Going where my attention brought me, I completed my masters in mechanical engineering on the robotics and automation track.

My current work, for the National Laboratory of the Rockies' Hydrogen Sensor Laboratory, focuses on the development and testing of frontier sensing and monitoring methods for hydrogen infrastructure. My role involves developing CV/ML systems, GPU programming, data analytics, and control system design/ implementation.
